Guideline for Annotators

Your Task: You are evaluating the therapeutic quality of a psychotherapy dialogue between a Therapist and a Client.

  • Therapist: Uses MI counseling skills (e.g., reflections, open questions etc.) to help the client explore and resolve ambivalence.
  • Client: Shares thoughts, feelings, and experiences.
Evaluation Criteria. Score ranges from 1 (Very Poor) to 5 (Excellent).
  • Coherence (Conversation):Is the conversation logically structured, with smooth transitions between steps? Look for logical order, no abrupt topic jumps, smooth flow.
  • Depth (Conversation): Does the conversation go beyond surface-level discussion and explore concepts in sufficient detail? Consider how deeply both roles explore the topic.
  • Progress (Conversation): Does the conversation effectively move forward through the tutorial steps in a logical manner? Check if each turn moves things forward rather than stagnating or repeating.
  • Naturalness (Conversation): Does the conversation feel fluid and human-like, avoiding robotic or overly scripted responses? Look for a natural tone, varied phrasing, and engaging dialogue.
  • Empathy (Therpiast): Does the therapist convey accurate understanding and acceptance of the client's perspective?
  • MI Alignment (Therapist): How well does the therapist adhere to Motivational Interviewing principles?
  • MI Alignment (Client): How realistically does the client respond according to MI principles?

MI Code Definitions

Therapist Codes

  • Reflection: Reflection is a statement made by the therapist that captures and mirrors back the essence of what the client has said or expressed.
  • Question: Made by the therapist to gain more clarity or to explore the client’s perspective, feelings, thoughts, or experiences.
    • Open Question: Therapist asks a question that allows for elaboration.
    • Closed Question: Therapist asks a question that invites a yes/no or brief answer.
  • Therapist Input: Any other therapist utterance that is not codable as 'question' or 'reflection'.
    • Information: Therapist provides factual or explanatory information.
    • Advice: Therapist gives suggestions or recommendations.
    • Giving Options: Therapist presents different choices or courses of action.
    • Negotiation / Goal-Setting: Therapist engages in collaborative planning or goal-setting.

Client Codes

  • Change Talk: Client language that supports positive behavior change and reflects motivation aligned with their values and goals.
  • Sustain Talk: Client language that expresses resistance to change and preference to maintain the status quo.
  • Neutral: Client language that does not clearly indicate a tendency toward or against change.
